ABC Market

Grocery eCommerce App

Strategy

With concerns about customer retention as well as competitors going digital, our (hypothetical) client, ABC Market, has determined to offer their customers the possibility to purchase groceries online and have them delivered to their doorstep.

I worked on a team of three within a two-week timeframe based on Google Venture's design sprint methodology. After synthesizing the data and multiple rounds of ideation exercises, we decided to create:

  • an e-commerce platform that reflects the image of ABC Market's premium brand,
  • order tracking, a feature that users would likely expect to see with an e-commerce platform, and
  • a system of reviews as our main distinguishing feature.

    • Visual Design

    Our team wanted to nail a specific look and feel that communicates our brand attributes: local, artisinal, and premium. We decided to go for a mostly dark theme, with pops of green to fit the users' mental model of grocery chains with fresh produce. A combination of serif and sans serif typography felt both modern and elevated, while large, editorial photography conveyed curated, high-quality taste.

    I created a style tile to guide our design between desirabily tests. Then, to ensure consistency across the various screens, I simultaneously updated a UI Style Guide with atomic design elements.

    Prototype

    My team agreed to divide and conquer the final prototype. I designed the high fidelity prototype for all shop, order status, and rate shopper screens while my teammates worked on the basket and checkout screens.

    • Prototype

    Next Steps

    Due to time constraints, we weren't able to build out a feature for product reviews during this sprint. Another feature that we'd like to include in the future are shopping or favorites lists for more efficient purchasing and re-purchasing.

    Finally, we'd be interested in measuring the success of our design by tracking such metrics as revenue, customer retention, and ratings for the app.
