Regimen

Skincare Product Tracker + Reviews

"Regimen" is an app to track and review skincare products for people looking to improve their skincare routine. It's like Foursquare or GoodReads -- for skincare products. For this project, I worked on my own within a two-week design sprint.


Why Skincare?

"It's not vanity, it's self-care." - Jonathan Van Ness, Queer Eye

Skincare is a relatively recent interest of mine. Growing up as a nerdy tomboy, I was raised to believe that one either had brains or beauty -- and that I had to choose between the two. Even now, many people often find skincare to be unapproachable -- it was too feminine, too uppity, too confusing. I felt it was time for a refresh on the skincare industry.

My business goals were to:

  • Rebrand skincare to be more welcoming
  • Create a tool for people who need help with their skincare routines
  • And, of course, monetize

With that, I filled out a Lean UX Canvas to help me come up with a few hypotheses, along with corresponding business outcomes that could gauge the success of the app.

The Solution

During the ideation process, I considered how I might...

  • ...make user reviews and logs more immersive (and not like filling out a spreadsheet)?
  • ...make ingredients info more accessible (and not "medicinal")?

I then prioritized my various feature ideas using the MOSCOW method, taking into consideration impact vs effort. From there, I determined what the minimum viable product would consist of.

Visual Design

This is the mood board. Brand attributes were trendy, cheerful, friendly.

I conducted five desirability tests on the mood board by asking users to describe the app, using Jennifer Aaker's Brand Personality Framework as a reference.

  • 3/5 described it as "trendy"
  • 3/5 described it as "young"
  • One described it as "cool but kind of corporate".

Based on this feedback, I decided to include bolder colors and rounder design elements achieve a friendlier mood.
